Overview of Malta

Malta is a small island off the coast of Italy. Malta floats alone in the middle of the Mediterranean Sea. Here the official language is English. Malta is known for its narrow streets, Italian food and unpredictable weather.

Top Places to Visit

Popeye Village

A must-see is Popeye Village. This is the set for the 1980 film. There you can feel like a real movie hero.

Blue Lagoon

Famous for its crystal-clear waters, ideal for swimming and snorkeling.

Marsaxlokk

A traditional fishing village with colorful boats and a vibrant Sunday market.

Transport Options

  • By Air: Malta International Airport near Valletta serves as the main gateway.
  • Ferries: Regular ferries connect Malta with Gozo and Comino.
  • Buses: An extensive bus network operates throughout the islands.
  • Taxis: Available but relatively expensive compared to buses.
  • Car Rentals: Widely available and a good option for exploring at your own pace

Pricing

  • Accommodation: Budget accommodations range from $15-40 per night in hostels and guesthouses. Mid-range hotels $60-120. Luxury hotels $150 and above.
  • Food: Street food and casual dining $5-15 per meal. Mid-range restaurants $20-40. High-end dining $50 and above.
  • Transport: Bus fares are around $1.50-3 per trip. Ferry trips to Gozo approximately $5-10. Taxi rides within cities $10-30, depending on distance.
